Cost-benefit analysis of tobacco consumption in Perú (2020). Serie Documentos de trabajo [LINK here]
(With Carlos De Los Rios and Dessire Medina)

The purpose of this study is to estimate the distributive effects of tobacco tax increases using the extended cost benefit analysis developed by Fuchs and Meneses (2017), which, in addition to the direct, short-term effects associated with decreased cigarette use, incorporates medium-and long-term effects of reduced cigarette smoking in terms of avoided medical expenses and increase in working years.

The impact of the 2017 El Niño Phenomenon on the academic performance of students in the Northern Coast of Perú

The general objective of the study is to analyze the impact of the 2017 El Niño Phenomenon on the educational outcomes of students in the school stage located in the northern departments of Peru, whose educational centers have been devastated by this climatic phenomenon. The specific objectives are divided into three parts: (i) to evaluate the total contribution of the phenomenon to the academic performance of the students, (ii) to compare the effects of the phenomenon at the department, natural region, and urban-rural area levels, and (iii) to find evidence of differentiated effects by gender.

Assessing the Impact of Conditional Cash Transfers on Forest Conservation: An Evaluation of the National Forest Conservation Program in Perú

The project aims to assess the impact of Conditional Cash Transfers (CCTs) on forest cover loss using a Difference-in-Differences (DID) model and satellite imagery data from Landsat 7 ETM+.
