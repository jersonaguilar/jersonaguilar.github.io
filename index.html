Welcome! I'm a researcher at the Instituto de Estudios Peruanos (IEP). My main interests are quantitative research, with a focus on causal inference, in topics related to education, migration, environment, health economics, development economics, and Spatial Econometrics.

Formerly, I worked as Economic Research's Analyst at the Cámara de Comercio de Lima and Economics Consultant for UNESCO, International Organization for Migration (IOM), Committee on Sustainability Assessment (COSA), Aluna Sustainability in Colombia, and for the Peruvian Government in the Ministry of Health and the Ministry of Economy and Finance.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Economics from Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos, a specialization diploma in Impact Evaluation of Social Programs and Public Policies from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile.

Currently, I am seeking to apply to a Ph.D. program in Economics and, in the long term, to dedicate myself to academic research.
